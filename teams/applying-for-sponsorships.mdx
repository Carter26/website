Applying for a sponsorship on GS Advertising & Connections takes just a few minutes, but the quality of your application has a direct impact on your acceptance rate. This guide walks you through the full application process and shares proven tips for writing applications that businesses respond to.

## How to apply

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a listing that fits your team">
    Browse the marketplace and use the filters to identify a sponsorship opportunity that matches your sport, location, and needs. Check the available spots to confirm the listing is still open.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the full listing">
    Click on the listing to view its complete details, including the sponsorship amount, description, benefits, and any specific requirements the business has set.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the business's requirements">
    Read the application requirements carefully. Note how many spots are available and whether the business has called out anything specific they want applicants to address.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Apply and open the application form">
    Select the **Apply** button on the listing page. This opens the application form tied to that specific opportunity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your application">
    Complete the application form with your team's information. Use this space to tell your team's story, explain why you need this sponsorship, describe exactly how the funds will be used, and make clear what the business receives in return for supporting your team.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit and confirm">
    Submit your completed application. You'll receive a confirmation notification immediately, and your application will appear in your dashboard under its current status.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Writing a winning application

A strong application is specific, genuine, and makes the business feel like they're investing in something real. Keep these principles in mind:

- **Be specific about your team** — name your sport, age group, and exactly how many athletes benefit from the sponsorship
- **Explain how funds will be used** — break down costs like equipment, tournament entry fees, uniforms, or travel so the business understands the impact of their contribution
- **Describe what the business gets in return** — spell out the value: logo placement on jerseys, a banner at home games, social media shoutouts, or mentions at events
- **Share your team's story** — include details about your coaches, how long the team has been together, your community ties, and highlights from past seasons
- **Keep it professional and sincere** — write as you would to a community partner, not a form you're filling out; businesses can tell the difference

<Tip>
  Specific, genuine applications consistently outperform generic ones. If something about a particular business stood out to you — their reputation in the community, their product, or their own connection to youth sports — mention it. Businesses respond to applicants who did their homework.
</Tip>

## Application statuses

Track every application from your dashboard. Each application displays one of the following statuses:

- **Pending** — your application has been submitted and is awaiting review by the business
- **Accepted** — the business accepted your application; you'll receive their contact information to coordinate sponsorship details
- **Declined** — the business passed on your application; the listing may still have open spots if you want to reapply or explore similar opportunities
- **Saved** — the business saved your application for later review and hasn't made a final decision yet

## After acceptance

When a business accepts your application, reach out to them promptly. Use the contact information provided to introduce yourself, confirm the sponsorship details, and get the arrangement formalized. Acting quickly shows the business they made the right choice.

<Note>
  You can apply to unlimited sponsorship opportunities at no cost. There are no limits on the number of active applications you can have at one time.
</Note>
